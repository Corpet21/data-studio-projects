Occupational accidents

Black people are more likely to get physically attacked at work, according to data

And hitting, kicking, beating, shoving are among the most common types of attack.

By Corina Petridi

Published Month XX, 2019

882.730 workers stayed home recovering from an injury or illness in 2017 in the U.S., according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. All of them were -at the moment- working for the private sector.

The wholesale trade industry appears to be the industry where a non-fatal injury is more likely to happen. There are nearly 15 events every 1.000 people working for the industry. The Transportation and Warehouse Industry comes second with almost 13 accidents happening every 1000 workers. On the contrary, Finance and Insurance and Educational services appear to be safer workplaces.

Source:Bureau of Labor Statistics

White Vs Non-White workers

The data also showed that Non-White people well incredibly much more likely to get injured while working. In particular, 120 per 1000 Non-white people experienced an occupational accident during 2017. That was definitely not the case for White people, since only 20 per 1000 workers suffer the same fate.

Source:Bureau of Labor Statistics

The racial breakdown

The Wholesale Trade industry, as mentioned before, is certainly the most dangerous one when it comes to non-fatal occupational accidents. But it's not equally dangerous for everyone. Black and Hispanic/Latino people tend to get more frequently injured, as highlighted in the graph below. More than 15 per 1000 Black and 1000 Hispanic/Latino people working for the Wholesale Trade Industry got hurt in 2017. The numbers are alarming for the Hispanic/Latino people working in Construction as well. On the contrary, White and Asian workers seem to be generally less vulnerable to workplace injuries.

Source:Bureau of Labor Statistics

Intentional VS Non-Intentional Injuries

According to data, Hispanic or Latino people were more frequently involved in a non-intentional accident in 2017. That means that the injury was not a result of another person's aggression. Asians were injured the least.On the other hand, Black workers were the most common target of attacks and assaults in 2017.And hitting, kicking, beating, shoving were the most common ways that they got attacked.

Source:Bureau of Labor Statistics

Note: The accidents for whom the race was not reported, were not included in the story.
